CUAsia Special Lecture Series The Legacy of C. C. Wang

Tuesday, February 26, 7pm

Lerner Hall, Room 569

 

To register online: http://goo.gl/AP2pJ

 

C. C. Wang was a celebrated collector and connoisseur of Chinese literati paintings. His collection of classical Chinese paintings is consistently listed as one of the greatest outside China. Close to 70 paintings formerly in the C.C. Wang's Collection are now in the Metropolitan Museum permanent collection. For over forty-years, C.C. Wang studied the paintings from the Imperial as well as other collections. These detailed annotations open the door to the inner thoughts of a formidable collector, demonstrating his expertise and thought process as a connoisseur of Chinese literati painting. This collection of private notes have been organized and translated into English by Kathleen Yang. And we are delighted to have her speak about her book, Through a Chinese Connoisseur’s Eye: Private Notes of C.C. Wang.

 

Kathleen Yang, a graduate of Harvard University, was first introduced to C.C. Wang in 1962 by her mother, when he was asked to make an authoritative evaluation of three paintings belonging to the family. Yang then studied with C.C. Wang for many decades to understand the traditional connoisseurship of Chinese classical painting, a system that has existed for many centuries in China, where one’s knowledge is transmitted from mentor to mentee.

 

Several copies of the book will be available for purchase and signing at the end of the event.

 

If you have any questions regarding the event, please contact cuasia.event@gmail.com.
